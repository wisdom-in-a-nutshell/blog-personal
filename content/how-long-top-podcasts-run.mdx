Part two of my podcast data deep dive. After analyzing [how often top podcasts publish](/top-podcast-cadence), I got curious about episode lengths.

So I pulled duration data for the same 1,000 shows and crunched the numbers.

I found everything from 6-minute daily snippets to 4+ hour conversations.

First I show the results, then methodology, then some thoughts on what this means.

### What Stood Out

- **Daily shows run shorter.** Average 47 minutes vs 61 minutes for weekly shows. Makes senseâ€”easier to fill an hour once a week than every day.

- **The extremes are extreme.** PragerU averages 6.4 minutes. Sacred Symbols averages 261 minutes (4+ hours). Both in the top 1%.

- **Weekly shows have the biggest range.** From 6-minute snippets to 4-hour deep dives. Weekly gives creators more flexibility to match content to length.

- **Monthly shows cluster around the middle.** Average 59 minutes. Seems like when you publish less frequently, you aim for that "substantial but not overwhelming" length.

### The Outliers

**Shortest averages (with enough episodes to be reliable):**
- PragerU 5-Minute Videos: 6.4 min
- Fr. Mike Schmitz Catholic Podcast: 6.9 min
- The Indicator from Planet Money: 8.9 min

**Longest averages:**
- Sacred Symbols PlayStation Podcast: 261 min (4.4 hours!)
- Lex Fridman Podcast: 218 min (3.6 hours)
- H3 Podcast: 209 min (3.5 hours)

## Methodology

Here's how I measured episode lengths:

- Started with the same top 1,000 podcasts by audience from my [cadence analysis](/top-podcast-cadence)
- All 1,000 shows had enough recent episodes with duration data
- Looked at up to 40 recent episodes per show
- Used mean duration per show (not median) since I wanted to capture their typical episode length
- Grouped by the same publishing frequency buckets: Daily, Near-daily, Weekly, Monthly, Other

*Publishing frequency buckets: Same as my previous analysis - Daily (~1 day), Near-daily (â‰¤3 days), Weekly (3-9 days), Monthly (10-29 days), Other (>30 days)*

That's it. Simple enough that anyone can reproduce this.
